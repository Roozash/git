spontaneous change

when a reaction does not need any external energy to continue is spontaneous procces

always need energy to contineu is non-spontaneous

First Law :the energy is conserved does not predict the direction of spontaneous change

Enthalpy H

Endothermic

The heat Enter (endo) the system and the Enthalpy is positive

Exothermic

The heat exit (exo) the system and the Enthalpy is negative

Entropy S

the measurment of randomness

Microstates

The number of possible ways energy can be distributed in a system

More Microstates mean higher Entropy

Second Law: The Entropy of the universe(system + surroundings) always increase in spontaneous process

factors affecting Entropy

  • Tempreture

    • Directly proportional

  • physical state

    • increase from solid to gas

  • atomic size and complexity

    • larger and more complex atoms have higher Entropy

  • solution formation

    • Dissolution generally increase Entropy

Third law

The Entropy goes to zero when tempreture drops to absolute zero

Gibbs

Formula with both Enthalpy and Entropy to predict spontaneity of a process

\(\Delta G_{\text{sys}} = \Delta H_{\text{sys}} - T\Delta S_{\text{sys}}\)

\(\Delta G\) < 0 : spontaneous process

\(\Delta G\) > 0 non spontaneous process

\(\Delta G\) = 0 Equilibrium
